Why use the functional form of setState?
this.setState(prevState => ({
   show: !prevState.show
}));

Answer

"Because setState is asynchronous. Using the previous state ensures the update is based on the latest value."


<section> is a semantic HTML tag. It gives meaning to your content, while <div> is just a generic container.

<div> vs <section>
❌ <div>
<div>
  <h2>User Profile</h2>
  <p>Bio...</p>
</div>
No meaning.
Just groups elements.
✅ <section>
<section>
  <h2>User Profile</h2>
  <p>Bio...</p>
</section>
Represents a thematic section of a page.
Gives meaning to browsers, screen readers, and search engines.
Benefits of <section>
1. Better Accessibility ⭐

Screen readers understand that this is a separate section of the page.

Example:

Profile Section
----------------
Name
Bio

Instead of just reading a generic <div>.

2. Better SEO ⭐

Search engines understand the page structure better.

Example:

<main>
    <section>
        <h2>Latest Blogs</h2>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Popular Recipes</h2>
    </section>
</main>

Google can better understand that these are different content sections.

3. Better Readability ⭐

When another developer reads your code:

<section>

they immediately know

"This is a section of the page."

Instead of

<div>

which gives no clue.

When should you use <section>?

Use it when the content has its own heading or purpose.
